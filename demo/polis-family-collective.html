Their Story

The Beaumonts didn't want
to argue about money anymore.

It started the same way it always does in big families. Somebody wasn't sure who was covering the turkey. Someone assumed someone else had booked the beach house. The argument after the reunion about who owed who for the cabin was still not resolved. And nobody — absolutely nobody — could remember who had agreed to the Costco run.

Grandma Beaumont, eighty-one years old and the undisputed matriarch of the family, looked around her Thanksgiving table and said: "We need a system. A real one."

"I didn't raise this family to argue over twenty dollars. We are going to write it down, we are going to vote, and we are going to stick to it."
— Eleanor "Grandma Bea" Beaumont, Founding Elder

Her granddaughter Jasmine, a product manager from Austin, set up a POLIS organization over the Christmas break. By New Year's Day, the family had a founding charter with seven articles, a treasury with five distinct funds, and twenty-four signatures on the founding document.

Three years later, Thanksgiving hosts haven't bickered about costs once. The 2026 reunion is already fully funded. The family voted 18–4 to go to Cancún. And that Costco run? Governed. Accountable. Split perfectly every time.

Founding Charter
The Beaumont Family Collective
v1.0.0 · Ratified January 1, 2022 · 24 founding signatures
Article I — Our Bond
This Collective exists to organize the shared life of the Beaumont family — our celebrations, our finances, our resources, and our time together — with fairness, transparency, and the kind of accountability that keeps love intact.
Article II — Annual Dues & The Five Funds
Each household contributes $50/month to the Collective Treasury, allocated across five funds: Thanksgiving (20%), Reunion (30%), Vacation (20%), Events (15%), and Bulk Purchasing (15%).
Article III — Democratic Decisions
All spending decisions above $200 require a member vote. Thanksgiving host selection, reunion location, vacation destination, and bulk purchase approvals all require simple majority. Charter amendments require two-thirds.
Article IV — The Reunion
A full family reunion shall be held every three years. Location is voted on 18 months in advance. The Reunion Fund collects monthly for three years before each event. No reunion may be planned that exceeds the Reunion Fund balance at booking time.
Article V — Governance & The AI
The POLIS Governance Engine enforces this Charter. It manages fund allocation, vote scheduling, split calculations, and SAR logging. It cannot override member votes. Every action it takes is visible to all members in real time.
