Their Story

Built on a Sunday afternoon,
funded by Monday morning.

It started with a question that Adaeze Okonkwo had been sitting on for months. Her grandmother had told her about the ajo — the rotating savings circle that had built her family's first house in Lagos. Simple math, shared discipline, radical trust. Adaeze wondered if something like that could work here, for people like them, with the same spirit but with rules that could hold up at scale.

She brought it up at her brother Emeka's Fourth of July cookout. By the time the brisket was done, six people had pulled out their phones to run the math. By Monday morning, they had a group chat. By the following Sunday, they had a constitution.

"We didn't need a bank to believe in us. We needed to believe in each other — and have something written down to remind us of that when things got hard."
— Adaeze Okonkwo, Founding Member & Circle President

Using POLIS, they drafted their founding charter in a single afternoon. The AI asked them the right questions: What are your values? Who gets a vote? What happens if someone misses a payment? What if someone needs their payout early? Every answer became a law. Every law became enforceable.

Twelve people. Four states. Two generations. Three family names. One shared understanding that wealth is not built alone — and that trust, properly structured, is the most valuable asset any community can hold.

Founding Charter
The Okonkwo-Williams Circle
Version 1.0.0 · Ratified January 14, 2024 · 12 founding signatures
Article I
Purpose & Bond
This Circle exists to multiply the financial power of its members through shared saving, mutual accountability, and democratic self-governance. We hold that wealth built together endures longer than wealth built alone.
Article II
Membership & Trust
Membership is by invitation and approval of two-thirds of the Circle. All members are equal in vote. No member may hold more than one position in any vote. Trust is the entry fee; the constitution is the receipt.
Article III
The Rotation
Each cycle, one member receives the full pool. Order is determined by founding lottery. Positions may be traded by mutual written consent. Hardship claims are reviewed and voted upon by the full Circle.
Article IV
The Legacy Fund
Eight percent of every payout is held in the Legacy Fund. These funds belong to the Circle in perpetuity and may only be distributed by a three-quarters vote. The Legacy Fund is a gift to the future members of this Circle.
Article V
Governance & The AI
The POLIS Governance Engine enforces this Charter impartially. It may not override member votes. It may not spend funds. Every action it takes is logged in the permanent SAR record and visible to all members.
